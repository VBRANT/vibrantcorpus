The Summer Warbler or Summer Yellow-bird, by which names this species is known in North America, is one of the commonest of Mniotiltidæ, and in the summer season spreads itself throughout the whole North-American continent, and breeds over an area extending from the confines of the Arctic Ocean to the frontier of Mexico, and from the Atlantic to the Pacific Oceans23. During the winter months it passes southwards to Mexico, Central America, and the whole of the northern portion of South America from Guiana to Ecuador, and is one of the most abundant of its family in Guatemala and elsewhere at this time of year, ranging from the sea-level to a height of 5000 or 6000 feet. D. æstiva has not yet been detected in any of the West-India Islands (except Trinidad), not even as a bird of passage, though many of these islands lie directly in the path the birds of the Eastern States must take when flying to the northern part of South America.
